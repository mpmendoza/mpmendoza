About Me

I'm an NSF Graduate Research Fellow and PhD student in biomedical engineering at Columbia University, with a focus on immunoengineering and DNA nanotechnology. I draw on two decades of leadership in tech and the nonprofit world to inform my approach to scientific research. Trained in molecular biology, cellular assays, and in vivo models—with a foundation in bioengineering from UC Berkeley—I’m especially drawn to work at the intersection of biology, engineering, and computation.

I thrive in fast-paced, collaborative environments where I can learn quickly, take ownership of complex systems, and contribute to bold, long-term goals. Whether building scalable solutions, mentoring emerging leaders, or working at the bench, I bring curiosity, adaptability, and a cross-disciplinary approach to problem-solving.
